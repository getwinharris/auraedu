**Kundli matching** (also known as Guna Milan or Ashtakoota matching) is the traditional Vedic system of assessing marriage compatibility. Rooted in ancient wisdom, this system evaluates 36 points (gunas) across eight categories to determine the harmony between prospective partners.

## Why Kundli Matching Matters

Marriage in Vedic tradition is not just a union of two individuals — it's a union of two souls, families, and karmic paths. Kundli matching helps:

- Identify potential areas of conflict
- Ensure emotional, physical, and spiritual compatibility
- Predict the overall happiness and longevity of the relationship
- Time the marriage for the most favorable planetary alignment

## The 8 Categories of Guna Milan

### 1. Varna (1 Guna)
Caste compatibility — represents spiritual compatibility and ego matching. It ensures both partners have compatible temperaments.

### 2. Vashya (2 Gunas)
Mutual attraction and control dynamics. Determines who will be more dominant in the relationship and whether both partners can influence each other positively.

### 3. Tara (3 Gunas)
Birth star compatibility — assesses the wellbeing and longevity of the relationship. It checks if the couple's Janma Nakshatras are in harmony.

### 4. Yoni (4 Gunas)
Sexual compatibility and intimacy. This category examines the physical and instinctual connection between partners based on their birth stars' animal symbols.

### 5. Graha Maitri (5 Gunas)
Friendship between the planetary lords of the couple's Moon signs. This determines natural affinity and mutual understanding.

### 6. Gana (6 Gunas)
Temperamental compatibility based on the three Ganas: Deva (godly), Manushya (human), and Rakshasa (demonic). Harmonious Gana matching ensures emotional compatibility.

### 7. Bhakoot (7 Gunas)
Emotional connection and family harmony. This is considered one of the most important categories — disharmony here can lead to serious conflicts.

### 8. Nadi (8 Gunas)
Health and genetic compatibility. Nadi matching is crucial because incompatible Nadi types are believed to cause health issues in children.

## Understanding the Score

- **32–36 Gunas**: Excellent compatibility — a highly recommended match
- **28–31 Gunas**: Good compatibility — minor adjustments needed
- **20–27 Gunas**: Average compatibility — can work with effort and remedies
- **Below 20 Gunas**: Low compatibility — marriage may face significant challenges

A minimum of 18 gunas is traditionally considered the threshold for a viable match, though exceptions can be made for otherwise strong charts.

## Beyond the Score: Nadi Dosha

Nadi Dosha occurs when both partners share the same Nadi type (Adi, Madhya, or Antya). This is considered a serious incompatibility that can affect children and family health. However, there are exceptions and remedies:

- If other aspects of the chart are exceptionally strong
- If either partner's 5th house is strong
- Through specific remedial rituals prescribed by an experienced astrologer
