## 2. Adjacent Alternative-Medicine & Allied-Health Training

- **AYUSH systems (BAMS/Ayurveda, BHMS/Homeopathy, BUMS/Unani, BNYS/Naturopathy & Yoga):** government-recognised,
  UGC/AICTE-aligned, often NEET-based. These are *not* direct substitutes for electropathy but compete for the
  same "alternative medicine career" aspirant.
- **Acupuncture training (M.D. Acupuncture, diploma/certificate courses):** offered by a mix of mainstream medical
  colleges and alternative-medicine institutes; B.E.M.S. syllabi commonly include Acupuncture as a subject.
- **Paramedical / allied-health councils:** large number of state-level paramedical institutes; relevant for
  Aura's "placements" and "allied health" messaging.

## 3. Recognition & Disclosure Context (CRITICAL)

Public career guides (IndCareer, Docthub) consistently note:
- B.E.M.S. / electropathy is **not currently recognised by the National Medical Commission (NMC)/MCI** as an
  allopathic-equivalent degree. It is treated as an **alternative / complementary medicine qualification**.
- Practice is generally as an **electropathy practitioner / consultant**, not as a registered allopathic "doctor"
  in official capacities.
- Government jobs for B.E.M.S. graduates are generally limited; private clinics, wellness centres, and independent
  practice are the main paths.

**Action for Aura:** the `approvals` and `disclosures` content model MUST state recognition status honestly. Do not
imply NMC/MCI recognition. Phrase as "electropathy / alternative-medicine programme" and disclose the affiliating
body Aura actually holds. Verify the exact affiliation wording with Aura's documents/Instagram before publishing.
